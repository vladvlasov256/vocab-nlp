A man from Deganwy in Conwy was very scared while waiting in a hospital corridor for 44 hours. He stayed at Ysbyty Glan Clwyd before doctors could remove his gallbladder. His gallbladder was very sick because it had gangrene. New information shows that almost 89,000 patients have been treated outside normal hospital rooms in recent years. This happened in hospitals run by Betsi Cadwaladr University Health Board. Many patients must wait in halls or corridors because there are not enough beds. This is a big problem for health care. Hospitals try to help many sick people every day. Some patients feel unsafe or nervous when they wait outside normal rooms. Health workers are very busy, and space in hospitals is sometimes not good for patients.
